**B.E.M**, which stands for **Block Element Modifier**, is a **naming convention** for **CSS** *classes*. It helps in creating *modular* and *maintainable* stylesheets. Here's a quick *cheatsheet* for **B.E.M**:

<hr className="mt-3 mb-5" />
### â–ªâ—¼ B.E.M Structure: â—¼â–ª 

1. **Block (*.block*):**
- Represents a high-level component or module.
- **Example:** *.navbar*, *.button*, *.card*

2. **Element (*.block__element*):**
- Represents a **part** of the block.
- Should be a child of the block.
- **Example:** *.navbar__item*, *.button__icon*, *.card__title*

3. **Modifier (*.block--modifier* or *.block__element--modifier*):**
- Represents a **different state** or version of the **block** or **element**.
- **Example:** *.button--disabled*, *.card__title--large*
